How to Prepare Your Pool for a Mermaid Visit

By Sarah Jenkins | May 12, 2026

Booking a mermaid for your child's pool party is a magical experience that will be remembered for years. However, to ensure the safety of our performers and the best possible experience for the children, a little bit of preparation goes a long way.

1. Water Temperature and Cleanliness

Mermaids are tough, but they are still human! Ensure that your pool is heated if the weather is chilly. A comfortable temperature helps the mermaid stay in the water longer. More importantly, ensure the pool is chemically balanced and clean. Crystal clear water makes for the best underwater photos!

2. Clear the Deck

Mermaids have beautiful, but heavy, silicone tails. Getting into the pool requires a clear pathway. Make sure to remove any tripping hazards like pool toys, hoses, or chairs from the area where the mermaid will be entering the water.

"A well-prepared pool ensures that the magic starts the second our mermaids arrive, with no delays!" - Marina the Mermaid

3. Have a Changing Area Ready

The transformation from human to mermaid requires privacy. Please have a ground-floor bathroom or a dedicated, private room near the pool area ready for the performer. They will need this space before and after their appearance.

4. Adult Supervision is Mandatory

While our mermaids lead games and keep the children entertained, they are not lifeguards. For safety reasons, we require at least one designated adult "water watcher" to be present at all times while the children are in the pool.

By following these simple steps, you set the stage for an enchanting and stress-free event. Ready to make a splash? Book a mermaid today!
